    Hardware Capture Evidence¶

    Archived serial-telemetry captures and bench evidence used to validate firmware behavior on real hardware. These are kept in the repo as gold-standard "evidence of fix" for major releases — each file is a deterministic record of what came off the device at a particular firmware version.

    How to add new captures¶

    Use the naming convention YYYY-MM-DD-vX.Y.Z-short-description.log so the filename sorts chronologically and is self-describing. Keep the file plain-text (serial dump or structured table) — easy to grep, diff, and review without special tooling.

    Add a row to the table above documenting what the capture shows and the release it informed.

    Why archive these in the repo¶

    These captures are small (< 2 KB each), evidence-heavy, and irreplaceable once the hardware is unwired or the firmware is overwritten. They're the permanent answer to "did the fix actually work on real silicon?" — much more valuable than chat transcripts or release-note prose, which describe what the captures show rather than being the captures themselves.
